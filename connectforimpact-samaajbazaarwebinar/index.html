2:30 PM - 4:15 PM IST

A diverse panel of experts and practitioners will explore how citizens across India are reimagining our cities from the “first mile”, where grassroots innovations emerge. They will draw on their experience and insights on driving volunteerism, participation, and citizen-driven change and will also share how communities can help ensure that municipal services truly reach every intended recipient.

Speaker: Rohini Nilekani, Author and philanthropist;
Founder-chairperson of Arghyam Foundation
Theme- Highlighting the role of Samaaj in the change-making process

3:05 PM - 4:10 PM

Panel Discussion

The root of the word ‘city’ comes from the Latin word ‘civis’, which means ‘citizen’. Citizens and local communities have always been at the heart of our cities, shaping new cultures and collectively solving problems. Citizens have also been pivotal to the quality of governance of our cities - driving local participation, mobilizing the communities through volunteerism and other forms of civic engagement. More recently, India is witnessing a new wave of imagination of our cities, led by the youth re-establishing a sense of trust and ownership in the citizens and keeping the government accountable. This panel of speakers will discuss their observations and experience on the phenomenon of citizens building and shaping the environs of our cities.

Panelists -

  • Kuldeep Dantewadia, CEO, Reap Benefit;
