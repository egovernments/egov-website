Keynote Speaker

Shri Ram Sewak Sharma

IAS (Retd), Former Chairperson, TRAI;
Former Director-General, UIDAI

Shri R S Sharma is one of India’s most prominent officials in the e-governance space, having initiated and directed various missions in his roles as Director-General UIDAI, Secretary MEITy, and most recently as Chairperson of TRAI. He has also served as Chief Secretary to the Government of Jharkhand. A strong proponent of the potential for technology to solve challenges in government, he holds an MSc. in Mathematics from IIT Kanpur, an MS in Computer Science from UC Riverside, and a PhD in Management and Public Policy from IIT Delhi. He is also the author of the recently published book “The Making of Aadhar”, and will reflect on lessons from the Aadhar experience.

Panel Discussion

India 4000+ cities are entering into an age where their capacities are not sufficient to respond to the challenge of rapid urbanisation. Technology and its sensible use and implementation is the need of the hour to augment the potential of available resources and bridge the supply-demand gap. The National Urban Innovation Stack (NUIS) approaches the same vision by empowering the urban ecosystem to solve a multitude of complex challenges at scale and speed. In the 21st century, India will embrace Digital Diplomacy as it designs and builds new public digital goods for itself and the world. This panel of speakers will discuss the potential of technology and e-governance in Urban India’s context and the role that the government will play in that journey.
